Figure R1 — DANCE outperforms the other methods on motor imagery datasets

Figure R1. DANCE shows precise performance on both localization and classification. Qualitative comparison of decoded event timelines on two motor imagery BCI datasets. Top: BNCI2014 (4-class: Tongue, Feet, Left Hand, Right Hand) showing a 70 s excerpt from a held-out test session. Bottom: BNCI2015 (2-class: Feet, Right Hand) showing a 300 s excerpt, illustrating performance at a longer time scale. Each row displays the ground-truth (GT) events and predictions from DANCE, CBraMod, and U-Net. Solid borders denote correct detections (prediction overlaps a same-class GT event by more than 50%), while dashed borders indicate false positives. Event-level F1 (F1e) and sample-level F1 (F1s) are reported on the right margin, computed over the full timeline.

Other Analysis

Figure R2 — Window size is determined by architectural constraints

Figure R2. The window size must balance two constraints: it should be large enough to capture the longest event, yet small enough for the model to reliably predict all events within it. F1 Event (IoU ≥ 0.5) is plotted as a function of window duration W, averaged across 5 cross-validation folds and 18 subjects. Two experimental settings are compared: (i) scaling the number of queries (Nq) proportionally with W, and (ii) fixing Nq = 150 while varying W.

Figure R3 — Onset-informed models fail to jointly detect and classify

Figure R3. DANCE outperforms EEGSym and ATCNet on BNCI2014 dataset. We evaluate two variants of EEGSym: classic, which produces a single class prediction per window and relies on timeline reconstruction to assign per-timestep labels, and dense, a minimal adaptation that outputs per-timestep predictions. We also added ATCNet, another strong model in the BCI literature, to show these models are inherently not designed to solve continuous event detection and classification. (A) F1 Sample (macro-averaged multiclass F1 over timesteps): DANCE achieves 50.8%, compared to 13.8%, 18.0%, and 15.2% for EEGSym classic, ATCNet, and EEGSym dense respectively (2.8–3.7× improvement). (B) F1 Event (IoU-F1@0.5 with class matching): DANCE reaches 39.8%, while all three baselines remain near zero (0.0%, 0.1%, 2.5%). Error bars denote SEM across 9 subjects.
