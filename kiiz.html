Main Analysis

Figure R1 — Model collapse and fragmented predictions on BNCI2014

Figure R1. Standard BCI classification and segmentation architectures either collapse or produce highly fragmented predictions when jointly tackling localisation and classification. Results on BNCI2014 (4 classes: Tongue, Feet, Left Hand, Right Hand) are shown for a 70 s excerpt from a held-out test session. Each row presents ground-truth (GT) events alongside predictions from DANCE, CBraMod, U-Net, and ATCNet. Solid borders indicate correct detections (predictions overlapping a same-class GT event by >50%), whereas dashed borders mark false positives. Event-level F1 (F1e) and sample-level F1 (F1s) are reported on the right, computed over the full sequence.

Figure R2 — Onset-informed models fail to jointly detect and classify

Figure R2. DANCE outperforms EEGSym and ATCNet on BNCI2014 dataset. We evaluate two variants of EEGSym: classic, which produces a single class prediction per window and relies on timeline reconstruction to assign per-timestep labels, and dense, a minimal adaptation that outputs per-timestep predictions. We also added ATCNet, another strong model in the BCI literature, to show these models are inherently not designed to solve continuous event detection and classification. (A) F1 Sample (macro-averaged multiclass F1 over timesteps): DANCE achieves 50.8%, compared to 13.8%, 18.0%, and 15.2% for EEGSym classic, ATCNet, and EEGSym dense respectively (2.8–3.7× improvement). (B) F1 Event (IoU-F1@0.5 with class matching): DANCE reaches 39.8%, while all three baselines remain near zero (0.0%, 0.1%, 2.5%). Error bars denote SEM across 9 subjects.

Figure R3 — No sign of overfitting for CBraMod model on BI2014b

Figure R3. F1 sample is stable during validation until the end of the training. It shows no sign of overfitting on the smallest dataset (BI2014b). F1 sample at validation is displayed across epochs on 5 different folds representing all subjects in the dataset.
