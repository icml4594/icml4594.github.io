Main Analysis

Figure R1 — DETR loss is stable across different weights combination

Figure R1. Performance of the DANCE model is stable across different weights combination. Scores are averaged across three datasets (BI2014b, BNCI2014, Broderick2019) using per-subject metrics aggregated over 5-fold cross-validation. Error bars denote standard error of the mean across datasets.

Figure R2 — Decoupled metric for localization and temporal precision

Figure R2. DANCE performs better on both metrics when the arrow points to the upper left. This plot shows a joint view of detection quality (F1detect, y-axis) and temporal precision (center prediction error in seconds, x-axis, log scale), comparing DANCE (colored circles) to the best non-ablation baseline (gray diamonds). F1det is a class-agnostic detection at IoU > 0.5 to quantify retrieval independent of classification. Δctr is the median center-point error in seconds, computed on matched events, to directly quantify temporal precision in physical units. Δctr is conditional on matching, and therefore must be interpreted jointly with F1 metrics. Note that Δctr depends on F1detect, as it is computed only on matched events.
